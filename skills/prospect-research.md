# Skill: Prospect Research

## Purpose
Conducts deep research on target practices and contacts to inform outreach strategy, personalize communications, and identify high-potential prospects.

## When to Use
- Before launching outreach to a new practice
- Preparing for a meeting or call
- Building targeted prospect lists for events
- Understanding a prospect's current offerings and potential BTL fit

## Inputs Required
- **Practice name and/or contact name**
- **Location** (city/state or ZIP)
- **Research depth**: Quick (5 min overview) or Deep (full intelligence report)
- **Purpose**: Why are we researching? (pre-call prep, event targeting, product fit assessment)

## Research Framework

### Quick Research (for pre-call or pre-email)
1. Practice website overview (services, team, locations)
2. Practice type and specialty confirmation
3. Current aesthetic/wellness offerings (do they already offer body contouring, skin treatments, etc.?)
4. Online presence (Google reviews rating, social media activity)
5. BTL product fit assessment

### Deep Research (for high-value targets)
All of the above, plus:
6. Key decision makers (names, roles)
7. Recent news or announcements
8. Competitive devices they currently use (if findable)
9. Patient demographics and volume indicators
10. Growth signals (new locations, new hires, expanded services)
11. Potential objections and talking points

## Output Format
```
---
PROSPECT INTELLIGENCE REPORT
Practice: [name]
Location: [city, state]
Type: [specialty]
Research Date: [date]
---

## Overview
[2-3 sentence summary]

## Key Decision Makers
- [Name, Title]

## Current Offerings
[What services they offer that are relevant to BTL]

## BTL Product Fit
- [Product]: [fit assessment and reasoning]

## Outreach Recommendations
- Best approach: [email/call/in-person]
- Key talking points: [personalized angles]
- Potential objections: [and how to address]

## Sources
[Where this info came from]
```

## Tools to Use
- ZoomInfo (contact enrichment) — when available via MCP
- Web search for practice websites and reviews
- HubSpot for existing contact history
- Google Maps for location/competition context

## Quality Checks
- [ ] Practice type is correctly identified
- [ ] Product fit assessment is realistic (not forcing a fit)
- [ ] Decision maker info is verified (not assumed)
- [ ] Outreach recommendations are actionable
- [ ] Sources are cited
