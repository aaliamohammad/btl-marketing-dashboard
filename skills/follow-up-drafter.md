# Skill: Follow-Up Drafter

## Purpose
Drafts contextual follow-up emails based on specific prospect interactions — calls, meetings, flyer views, event attendance, or prior email engagement.

## When to Use
- After a phone call or voicemail
- After a prospect views a flyer or clicks a link
- After a meeting or demo
- After an event interaction
- When a prospect replies but doesn't commit
- When a prospect declines (keep door open)

## Inputs Required
- **Trigger**: What happened? (call, flyer view, meeting, reply, decline, etc.)
- **Contact name**: First name (or "Hi there" for general office emails)
- **Practice name and type**: For personalization
- **What was discussed** (if applicable): Key points from the interaction
- **Next step goal**: What you want them to do next
- **Thread subject** (if replying): Must use exact "Re: [original subject]"

## Follow-Up Templates by Trigger

### Post-Call (Spoke with someone)
- Reference who you spoke with and what was discussed
- CRITICAL: Only reference the person you actually spoke with — if you only reached the front desk, do NOT imply you spoke with the doctor/owner
- Attach relevant materials mentioned in the call
- Clear next step (meeting invite, demo, event registration)

### Post-Call (Voicemail)
- Acknowledge you called and left a message
- Brief value prop in writing
- Offer alternative contact methods

### Post-Flyer-View / Link Click
- Acknowledge their interest without being creepy about tracking
- Expand on what they were looking at
- Offer to answer questions or set up a quick call

### Post-Meeting / Demo
- Thank them for their time
- Recap key points discussed
- Outline clear next steps with timeline
- Include any materials promised

### Post-Decline
- Respect their decision
- Keep it short and gracious
- Low-pressure ask for a brief intro meeting instead
- Leave the door open for future

### Post-Event
- Reference specific conversations or moments from the event
- Connect back to their practice's specific needs
- Propose concrete next step

## Style Rules
- Match the energy of the interaction (formal meeting = slightly more structured; casual call = conversational)
- NO em dashes
- Short paragraphs
- Always reference the specific interaction to show it's not a mass email
- Signature: Aalia Mohammad | Practice Success Manager | [phone] | [email]

## Quality Checks
- [ ] Accurately reflects what actually happened (no false attribution)
- [ ] Appropriate urgency level for the situation
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] Clear single CTA
- [ ] Maintains threading if it's a reply
